Privacy Policy

Penguin Protocol — last updated 2026-07-17

Penguin Protocol is a mobile game published by Bee Four Studio. This policy explains what data the app collects and how it is used.

No user accounts

Penguin Protocol does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide any personal information. All game progress is stored locally on your device and is not uploaded to any server.

Advertising (Google AdMob)

This app displays advertisements served by Google AdMob. Google AdMob may collect device identifiers (such as the Advertising ID on Android or IDFA on iOS), IP address, and coarse location data for advertising and analytics purposes. This data is collected by Google, not by Bee Four Studio, and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

You may be asked for consent to personalized advertising when you first open the app, depending on your region. You can reset your advertising identifier in your device settings at any time.

In-app purchases

Purchases are processed by Apple (App Store) or Google (Play Store) and are subject to their respective privacy policies. The app does not see your payment method.

Optional analytics

In a future update, the app may optionally use Firebase Analytics to understand how people play. If enabled, Firebase collects de-identified usage data (e.g., session length, features used). You will be notified before any analytics are activated.

Data stored on your device

The following is stored locally on your device using AsyncStorage and is never transmitted to any server: your game balance, bot states, card collection, streak progress, transaction history, donation history, and preferences (charity selection, haptics toggle). Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all of this data.

Children

This app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction (including CCPA in California and GDPR in the European Economic Area), you may have the right to access, delete, or opt out of the sale of personal data. Because the app stores all data on your device and does not create accounts, exercising these rights is done through your device settings (e.g., resetting your advertising identifier, uninstalling the app).
